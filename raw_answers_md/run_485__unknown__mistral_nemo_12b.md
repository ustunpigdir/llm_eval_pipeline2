**1. Solid angle $\Omega$ subtended by the cone:**

The solid angle $\Omega$ subtended by a cone of half-angle $\theta$ at the origin is given by the formula:

$$\Omega = 2\pi (1 - \cos \theta)$$

For $\theta = 60^\circ$,

$$\Omega = 2\pi (1 - \cos 60^\circ) = 2\pi (1 - \frac{1}{2}) = \pi$$

**2. Berry phase for spin-1/2:**

For a spin-1/2 particle in an adiabatically rotating field, the Berry phase is given by:

$$\gamma = \frac{\Omega}{2}$$

Substituting the value of $\Omega$ we found,

$$\gamma = \frac{\pi}{2}$$

**3. Berry phase for spin-1:**

For a spin-1 particle in an aligned state ($m = 1$), the Berry phase is given by:

$$\gamma = \frac{\Omega}{2S + 1}$$

where $S$ is the total spin. For $S = 1$,

$$\gamma = \frac{\pi}{2 \cdot 1 + 1} = \frac{\pi}{3}$$

**4. Geometric nature of the Berry phase:**

The Berry phase is geometric because it depends only on the topology of the path traced by the instantaneous eigenstates in Hilbert space, not on the details of the Hamiltonian or the dynamics. It is independent of the speed of the adiabatic evolution and the specific form of the Hamiltonian.
