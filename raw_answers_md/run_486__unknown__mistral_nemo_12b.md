**1. Compute the solid angle $\Omega$ subtended by the cone at the origin.**

The solid angle $\Omega$ subtended by a cone at the origin is given by the formula:
\[
\Omega = 2\pi (1 - \cos \theta)
\]
where $\theta$ is the half-angle of the cone. For $\theta = 60^\circ$,
\[
\Omega = 2\pi (1 - \cos 60^\circ) = 2\pi (1 - \frac{1}{2}) = \pi
\]

**2. State the Berry phase for a spin-1/2 aligned state in terms of $\Omega$, and evaluate its magnitude.**

For a spin-1/2 particle in an adiabatically rotating field, the Berry phase $\gamma$ is given by:
\[
\gamma = -\frac{1}{2} \Omega
\]
Substituting the value of $\Omega$ we found,
\[
\gamma = -\frac{1}{2} \times \pi = -\frac{\pi}{2}
\]

**3. How would the result change for total spin $S = 1$ (aligned, $m = 1$)?**

For a spin-1 particle in an adiabatically rotating field, the Berry phase $\gamma$ is given by:
\[
\gamma = -S \Omega
\]
where $S$ is the total spin. For $S = 1$ and $\Omega = \pi$,
\[
\gamma = -1 \times \pi = -\pi
\]

**4. Explain why this phase is geometric: what does it NOT depend on?**

The Berry phase is geometric because it depends only on the geometry of the path traced by the instantaneous eigenstates of the Hamiltonian, and not on the details of the Hamiltonian itself or the speed of the adiabatic evolution. It does not depend on the magnitude of the magnetic field, the specific form of the Hamiltonian, or the rate of rotation of the field.
