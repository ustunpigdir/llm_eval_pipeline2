**1. Hubble distance and linear term at z = 0.200**

The linear term at $z = 0.2$ is:
\[\frac{cz}{H_0} = \frac{0.2 \times 70.0 \text{ km/s/Mpc}}{70.0 \text{ km/s/Mpc}} = 0.2 \text{ Mpc}\]

**2. Luminosity distance for LCDM at z = 0.2**

For LCDM ($q_0 = -0.55$):
\[d_L = \frac{cz}{H_0}\left[1 + \frac{(1-q_0)z}{2}\right] = 0.2 \left[1 + \frac{(1-(-0.55)) \times 0.2}{2}\right] = 0.2 \times 1.15 = 0.23 \text{ Mpc}\]

**3. Luminosity distance for Einstein-de Sitter at z = 0.2**

For Einstein-de Sitter ($q_0 = +0.5$):
\[d_L = \frac{cz}{H_0}\left[1 + \frac{(1-q_0)z}{2}\right] = 0.2 \left[1 + \frac{(1-0.5) \times 0.2}{2}\right] = 0.2 \times 1.05 = 0.21 \text{ Mpc}\]
