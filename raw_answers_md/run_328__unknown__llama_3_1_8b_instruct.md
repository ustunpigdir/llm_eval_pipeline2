The Unruh effect is a phenomenon in which an accelerated observer in Minkowski spacetime perceives a thermal bath of particles, with a temperature given by the Unruh temperature formula:

$$T = \frac{\hbar a}{2\pi c k_B}$$

where $a$ is the proper acceleration of the observer, $\hbar$ is the reduced Planck constant, $c$ is the speed of light, and $k_B$ is the Boltzmann constant.

The physical statement of the effect is that an accelerated detector in the Minkowski vacuum will see a thermal radiation with this temperature, whereas an inertial observer will see no radiation.

To evaluate the Unruh temperature for $a = 10^{20}$ m/s$^2$, we need to plug in the values of the constants:

$$T = \frac{\hbar a}{2\pi c k_B} = \frac{(1.0545718 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}) (1.00 \times 10^{20} \text{ m/s}^2)}{2\pi (3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}) (1.38 \times 10^{-23} \text{ J/K})}$$

$$T = \frac{1.0545718 \times 10^{-14} \text{ J}}{2\pi (1.38 \times 10^{-23} \text{ J/K})} = 2.42 \times 10^{9} \text{ K}$$

However, this temperature is extremely high and is not relevant to everyday situations.

To find the proper acceleration that gives $T = 1.00$ K, we can rearrange the Unruh temperature formula:

$$a = \frac{2\pi c k_B T}{\hbar} = \frac{2\pi (3.00 \times 10^8 \text{ m/s}) (1.38 \times 10^{-23} \text{ J/K}) (1.00 \text{ K})}{1.0545718 \times 10^{-34} \text{ J s}}$$

$$a = \frac{2\pi (3.00 \times 10^8) (1.38 \times 10^{-23}) (1.00)}{1.0545718 \times 10^{-34}} = 1.32 \times 10^{20} \text{ m/s}^2$$

Therefore, the acceleration giving $T = 1$ K is $1.32 \times 10^{20}$ m/s$^2$.

The relation to Hawking radiation via the equivalence principle is that the Unruh effect is a consequence of the equivalence principle, which states that the effects of gravity are equivalent to the effects of acceleration. Hawking radiation is a consequence of the equivalence principle as well, and it is a result of the acceleration of the event horizon of a black hole. The Unruh effect is a more general phenomenon that occurs in any accelerating frame, whereas Hawking radiation is a specific consequence of the acceleration of the event horizon.

Everyday accelerations produce unobservably small $T$ because the Unruh temperature is inversely proportional to the acceleration. For example, the acceleration of a car on the highway is about $10^2$ m/s$^2$, which would give a temperature of about $10^{-12}$ K, which is much too small to be observed.
