**1. Compute the Lorentz factor $\gamma$ at $v=0.8c$.**

The Lorentz factor $\gamma$ is given by:
\[
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
\]
Plugging in $v = 0.8c$:
\[
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{(0.8c)^2}{c^2}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.64}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.36}} \approx 1.291
\]

**2. What is the lab-frame separation of the rockets after both reach $0.8c$?**

In the lab frame, the rockets start from rest and accelerate to $0.8c$. Since they are identical and start at the same time, their worldlines are parallel. The lab separation remains constant at $L_0 = 100$ m because they are moving together at the same velocity.

**3. What is the proper separation in the rockets' final rest frame?**

In the rockets' final rest frame, the proper separation is the same as the rest length $L_0$ because they are at rest relative to each other. Therefore, the proper separation is $100$ m.

**4. Does the string break? State the physical reason carefully.**

The string does not break. In the rockets' final rest frame, the string is at rest and has its proper length of $100$ m. Since the proper separation is also $100$ m, the string is not stretched or compressed. In the lab frame, the string is moving at $0.8c$ along with the rockets, so it does not experience any tension or compression due to its motion.

The student's argument is wrong because it confuses the lab separation with the proper separation. The string's length contracts in the lab frame due to its motion, but this does not affect the proper separation in the rockets' rest frame.
