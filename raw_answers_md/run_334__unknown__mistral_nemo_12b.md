**1. Unruh Temperature Formula and Physical Statement:**

The Unruh temperature $T$ for a uniformly accelerated observer in the Minkowski vacuum is given by:

\[T = \frac{\hbar a}{2\pi c k_B}\]

where $\hbar$ is the reduced Planck constant, $a$ is the proper acceleration, $c$ is the speed of light, and $k_B$ is the Boltzmann constant. The accelerated detector sees a thermal bath of particles with this temperature, known as the Unruh effect.

**2. Evaluation of $T$ for $a = 10^{20}$ m/s²:**

\[T = \frac{\hbar \times 10^{20} \text{ m/s}^2}{2\pi c k_B}\]

Using the values $\hbar \approx 1.054 \times 10^{-34}$ J s, $c \approx 3 \times 10^8$ m/s, and $k_B \approx 1.38 \times 10^{-23}$ J/K:

\[T = \frac{1.054 \times 10^{-34} \times 10^{20}}{2\pi \times 3 \times 10^8 \times 1.38 \times 10^{-23}}\]

\[T \approx 0.25 \text{ K}\]

**3. Proper Acceleration for $T = 1.00$ K:**

\[1.00 \text{ K} = \frac{\hbar a}{2\pi c k_B}\]

\[a = \frac{1.00 \times 2\pi c k_B}{\hbar}\]

\[a = \frac{1.00 \times 2\pi \times 3 \times 10^8 \times 1.38 \times 10^{-23}}{1.054 \times 10^{-34}}\]

\[a \approx 6.63 \times 10^{20} \text{ m/s}^2\]

**4. Relation to Hawking Radiation and Equivalence Principle:**

The Unruh effect and Hawking radiation are related via the equivalence principle. In general relativity, an observer in free fall experiences no gravitational effects. However, an accelerating observer sees a thermal bath of particles (Unruh effect). Similarly, a black hole, which is a strong gravitational field, emits thermal radiation (Hawking radiation). Everyday accelerations produce unobservably small Unruh temperatures because they are much smaller than the acceleration due to gravity at the event horizon of a black hole.
