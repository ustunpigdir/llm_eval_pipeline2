**1. Compute the Lorentz factor $\gamma$ at $v=0.8c$.**

The Lorentz factor $\gamma$ is given by:
\[
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{v^2}{c^2}}}
\]
Plugging in $v = 0.8c$:
\[
\gamma = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - \frac{(0.8c)^2}{c^2}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{1 - 0.64}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{0.36}} \approx 1.581
\]

**2. What is the lab-frame separation of the rockets after both reach $0.8c$?**

In the lab frame, the rockets start from rest and accelerate to $0.8c$. Since they are identical and start at the same time, their velocities are equal at every lab-frame instant. Therefore, the lab separation remains constant at $L_0 = 100$ m.

**3. What is the proper separation in the rockets' final rest frame?**

In the rockets' final rest frame, the proper separation is the distance between them measured in this frame. Since they are coasting at $0.8c$ relative to the lab frame, they are at rest relative to each other in their final frame. Thus, the proper separation is simply $L_0 = 100$ m.

**4. Does the string break? State the physical reason carefully.**

The string does not break. In the rockets' final rest frame, the string has a proper length of $100$ m, which is equal to the proper separation of the rockets. Therefore, the string is not stretched or compressed in this frame.

The student's argument is incorrect because it confuses the lab separation with the proper separation.
