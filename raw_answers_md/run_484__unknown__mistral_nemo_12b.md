**1. Solid angle $\Omega$ subtended by the cone:**

The solid angle $\Omega$ subtended by a cone of half-angle $\theta$ at the origin is given by the formula:

$$\Omega = 2\pi (1 - \cos \theta)$$

For $\theta = 60^\circ$,

$$\Omega = 2\pi (1 - \cos 60^\circ) = 2\pi (1 - \frac{1}{2}) = \pi \text{ steradians}$$

**2. Berry phase for spin-1/2:**

For a spin-1/2 particle in an adiabatically rotating field, the Berry phase is given by:

$$\gamma_B = \frac{\Omega}{2}$$

Substituting the value of $\Omega$ we found,

$$\gamma_B = \frac{\pi}{2} \text{ radians}$$

**3. Berry phase for spin-1 (aligned, $m = 1$):**

For a spin-1 particle in an aligned state ($m = 1$), the Berry phase is given by:

$$\gamma_B = \frac{\Omega}{2S}$$

where $S$ is the total spin. Substituting $S = 1$ and $\Omega = \pi$,

$$\gamma_B = \frac{\pi}{2 \times 1} = \frac{\pi}{2} \text{ radians}$$

**4. Geometric nature of Berry phase:**

The Berry phase is geometric because it depends only on the topology of the parameter space traced by the Hamiltonian and not on the details of the path or the speed of evolution. It is independent of the dynamics of the system and is a purely geometric property of the Hilbert space.
