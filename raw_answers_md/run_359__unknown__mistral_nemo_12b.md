**1. Yukawa Potential**

The Yukawa potential for the nuclear force mediated by pion exchange is given by:

\[ V(r) = -\frac{g^2}{4\pi r} \cdot \frac{e^{-m_\pi r}}{r} \]

where:
- \(g\) is the coupling constant,
- \(m_\pi\) is the mass of the pion,
- \(r\) is the distance between the nucleons.

The mediator mass \(m_\pi\) enters the exponential term, which describes the decay of the potential with distance.

**2. Reduced Compton Wavelength**

The reduced Compton wavelength is given by:

\[ \lambda = \frac{\hbar}{m_\pi c} \]

Using the given values \(m_{\pi^\pm} c^2 = 139.57\) MeV and \(\hbar c = 197.327\) MeV fm, we can calculate:

\[ \lambda = \frac{197.327 \text{ MeV fm}}{139.57 \text{ MeV}} \approx 1.42 \text{ fm} \]

**3. Non-Reduced Compton Wavelength**

The non-reduced Compton wavelength is given by:

\[ \Lambda = \frac{h}{m_\pi c} \]

Using the same values:

\[ \Lambda = \frac{6.5821 \times 10^{-22} \text{ MeV s}}{139.57 \text{ MeV}} \approx 4.74 \times 10^{-22} \text{ m} \approx 4.74 \times 10^{-13} \text{ fm} \]

This is much smaller than the reduced wavelength and is not the appropriate scale for the nuclear force range. The reduced wavelength should be used in the Yukawa exponential because it is the appropriate scale for the distance between nucleons.

**4. Mass Implied by a 2.0 fm Range**

If we assume the range of the nuclear force is 2.0 fm, we can solve for the mediator mass:

\[ \lambda = \frac{\hbar}{m_\pi c} = 2.0 \text{ fm} \]

\[ m_\pi = \frac{\hbar}{2.0 \text{ fm} \cdot c} \approx \frac{197.327 \text{ MeV fm}}{2.0 \text{ fm}} \approx 98.66 \text{ MeV} \]

This mass is much lower than the actual pion mass, indicating that the pion is not the mediator for a 2.0 fm range. Historically, Yukawa predicted a new particle (the pion) with a mass around 200 MeV to explain the nuclear force range.
