**1. Yukawa Potential**

The Yukawa potential for the nuclear force mediated by pion exchange is given by:

\[ V(r) = -\frac{g^2}{4\pi} \frac{e^{-m_\pi r}}{r} \]

where \( g \) is the coupling constant, \( m_\pi \) is the pion mass, and \( r \) is the distance between the nucleons. The mediator mass \( m_\pi \) enters the exponential term, determining the range of the force.

**2. Reduced Compton Wavelength**

The reduced Compton wavelength \( \lambda \) is given by:

\[ \lambda = \frac{\hbar}{m_\pi c} \]

Using the given values \( m_{\pi^\pm} c^2 = 139.57 \) MeV and \( \hbar c = 197.327 \) MeV fm, we can calculate \( \lambda \):

\[ \lambda = \frac{197.327 \text{ MeV fm}}{139.57 \text{ MeV/c}} = 1.421 \text{ fm} \]

**3. Non-reduced Compton Wavelength**

The non-reduced Compton wavelength \( \Lambda \) is given by:

\[ \Lambda = \frac{h}{m_\pi c} \]

Using the same values as before:

\[ \Lambda = \frac{6.582 \times 10^{-22} \text{ MeV s}}{139.57 \text{ MeV/c}} = 4.735 \times 10^{-23} \text{ m} = 4.735 \times 10^{-17} \text{ fm} \]

The non-reduced Compton wavelength is much smaller than the reduced one and does not represent the range of the Yukawa potential. The reduced Compton wavelength should be used in the Yukawa exponential because it represents the characteristic distance scale over which the potential decays.

**4. Mass Implied by a 2.0 fm Range**

If the range of the force is 2.0 fm, we can solve for the mediator mass \( m \) using the reduced Compton wavelength:

\[ \lambda = \frac{\hbar}{mc} = 2.0 \text{ fm} \]

\[ m = \frac{\hbar}{2.0 \text{ fm} \cdot c} = \frac{197.327 \text{ MeV fm}}{2.0 \text{ fm} \cdot c} = 98.6635 \text{ MeV} \]
