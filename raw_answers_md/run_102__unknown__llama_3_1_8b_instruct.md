## Step 1: Evaluate the Hubble time in Gyr
The Hubble time is given by $1/H_0$. We need to convert $H_0$ from km/s/Mpc to Gyr$^{-1}$.

First, we need to convert 1 Mpc to km:
$1 \text{ Mpc} = 3.0857 \times 10^{19} \text{ km}$

Then, we can convert $H_0$ from km/s/Mpc to Gyr$^{-1}$:
$H_0 = 70.0 \text{ km/s/Mpc} = 70.0 \text{ km/s} / (3.0857 \times 10^{19} \text{ km}) = 2.26 \times 10^{-18} \text{ s}^{-1}$

Now, we can calculate the Hubble time in Gyr:
$hubble\_time\_gyr = 1/H_0 = 1/(2.26 \times 10^{-18} \text{ s}^{-1}) = 4.43 \times 10^{17} \text{ s} = 1.39 \text{ Gyr}$

$lcdm\_age\_gyr = \frac{2}{3 \times 2.26 \times 10^{-18} \text{ s}^{-1} \sqrt{0.7}} \times \mathrm{asinh}\sqrt{0.7/0.3} = 13.8 \text{ Gyr}$
