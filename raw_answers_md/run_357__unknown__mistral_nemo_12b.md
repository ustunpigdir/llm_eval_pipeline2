**1. Yukawa Potential**

The Yukawa potential for the nuclear force mediated by pion exchange is given by:

\[ V(r) = -\frac{g^2}{4\pi} \frac{e^{-m_\pi r}}{r} \]

where \( g \) is the coupling constant, \( m_\pi \) is the pion mass, and \( r \) is the distance between the nucleons. The mediator mass \( m_\pi \) enters the exponential term, determining the range of the force.

**2. Reduced Compton Wavelength**

The reduced Compton wavelength \( \lambda \) is given by:

\[ \lambda = \frac{\hbar}{m_\pi c} \]

Substituting the given values:

\[ \lambda = \frac{197.327 \text{ MeV fm}}{139.57 \text{ MeV}} \approx 1.42 \text{ fm} \]

**3. Non-Reduced Compton Wavelength**

The non-reduced Compton wavelength \( \Lambda \) is given by:

\[ \Lambda = \frac{h}{m_\pi c} \]

Substituting the given values:

\[ \Lambda = \frac{1973.27 \text{ MeV fm}}{139.57 \text{ MeV}} \approx 14.2 \text{ fm} \]

The reduced Compton wavelength should be used in the Yukawa exponential because it is the natural scale for the de Broglie wavelength of the mediator particle in the non-relativistic limit. The non-reduced wavelength is not appropriate for the Yukawa potential as it does not account for the relativistic nature of the pion.

**4. Mass Implied by a 2.0 fm Range**

If the range of the force is 2.0 fm, we can find the implied mediator mass by setting the reduced Compton wavelength equal to the range:

\[ \lambda = \frac{\hbar}{m_\pi c} = 2.0 \text{ fm} \]

Solving for \( m_\pi \):

\[ m_\pi = \frac{\hbar c}{2.0 \text{ fm}} \approx 98.66 \text{ MeV} \]
