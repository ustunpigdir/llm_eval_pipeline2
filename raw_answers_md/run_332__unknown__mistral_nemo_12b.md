**1. Unruh temperature formula and physical statement:**

The Unruh temperature $T_U$ for a uniformly accelerated observer in the Minkowski vacuum is given by:

\[T_U = \frac{\hbar a}{2\pi c k_B}\]

where:
- $\hbar$ is the reduced Planck constant,
- $a$ is the proper acceleration,
- $c$ is the speed of light,
- $k_B$ is the Boltzmann constant.

The accelerated detector sees a thermal bath of particles with this temperature, known as the Unruh effect.

**2. Evaluation of $T_U$ for $a = 10^{20}$ m/s$^2$:**

\[T_U = \frac{\hbar \times 10^{20} \text{ m/s}^2}{2\pi c k_B}\]

Using the values $\hbar \approx 1.054 \times 10^{-34}$ J s, $c \approx 3 \times 10^8$ m/s, and $k_B \approx 1.38 \times 10^{-23}$ J/K:

\[T_U \approx \frac{1.054 \times 10^{-34} \times 10^{20}}{2\pi \times 3 \times 10^8 \times 1.38 \times 10^{-23}} \approx 0.25 \text{ K}\]

**3. Proper acceleration for $T_U = 1.00$ K:**

\[1.00 \text{ K} = \frac{\hbar a}{2\pi c k_B}\]

Solving for $a$:

\[a = \frac{1.00 \times 2\pi c k_B}{\hbar}\]

\[a \approx \frac{1.00 \times 2\pi \times 3 \times 10^8 \times 1.38 \times 10^{-23}}{1.054 \times 10^{-34}} \approx 6.63 \times 10^{20} \text{ m/s}^2\]

**4. Relation to Hawking radiation and everyday accelerations:**

The equivalence principle relates the Unruh effect to Hawking radiation. A black hole can be seen as an observer in free fall, accelerating away from the event horizon. Thus, the Hawking temperature is the Unruh temperature for an observer at the event horizon.

Everyday accelerations produce unobservably small Unruh temperatures because they are much smaller than the acceleration required to achieve the Unruh temperature of 1 K. For example, an astronaut in the International Space Station experiences an acceleration due to gravity of about $9.0 \times 10^{-3}$ m/s$^2$, which would give an Unruh temperature of about $2.2 \times 10^{-25}$ K.
