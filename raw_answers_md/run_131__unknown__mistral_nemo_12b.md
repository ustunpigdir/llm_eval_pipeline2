**1. Photon temperature scaling with redshift:**

The photon temperature scales inversely with the scale factor $a$ because the wavelength of the photons stretches as the universe expands. The temperature at redshift $z$ is given by:

\[T(z) = T_0 (1 + z)\]

At recombination ($z = 1100$), the temperature is:

\[T_{rec} = T_0 (1 + z_{rec}) = 2.725 \text{ K} \times (1 + 1100) = 2.725 \text{ K} \times 1101 \approx 3000 \text{ K}\]

**2. Photon number density scaling:**

The number density of photons scales as $a^{-3}$. Therefore, the ratio of the number density at recombination to today is:

\[\frac{n(z_{rec})}{n_0} = \left(\frac{a_0}{a_{rec}}\right)^3 = (1 + z_{rec})^3\]

Taking the logarithm base 10:

\[\log_{10} \left(\frac{n(z_{rec})}{n_0}\right) = \log_{10} (1 + z_{rec})^3 = 3 \log_{10} (1 + z_{rec}) = 3 \log_{10} 1101 \approx 3.03\]

**3. Photon energy density scaling:**

The energy density of photons scales as $a^{-4}$. Therefore, the ratio of the energy density at recombination to today is:

\[\frac{\rho(z_{rec})}{\rho_0} = \left(\frac{a_0}{a_{rec}}\right)^4 = (1 + z_{rec})^4\]

Taking the logarithm base 10:

\[\log_{10} \left(\frac{\rho(z_{rec})}{\rho_0}\right) = \log_{10} (1 + z_{rec})^4 = 4 \log_{10} (1 + z_{rec}) = 4 \log_{10} 1101 \approx 4.04\]

The extra factor of $(1+z)$ relative to number density comes from the fact that the energy of each photon also scales with $a^{-1}$, so the energy density scales as $a^{-4}$ instead of $a^{-3}$.

**4. Why the CMB remains a perfect blackbody:**

The CMB remains a perfect blackbody as it redshifts because the universe expands adiabatically, meaning no heat is added or removed. This means the photons and matter in the universe interact only through gravity, and the photons do not lose or gain energy. Since the CMB was last scattered at recombination, it has been traveling through the universe without any rethermalization.
