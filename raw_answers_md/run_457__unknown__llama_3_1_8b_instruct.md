## Step 1: Compute the enclosed flux Φ
The enclosed flux Φ is given by the product of the magnetic field B and the area enclosed by the electron paths. Since the solenoid has a radius r = 1.00 μm and the field is uniform inside the solenoid, the area enclosed is πr^2. Therefore, Φ = B × πr^2.

## Step 2: Calculate the enclosed flux Φ
Substitute the given values into the formula: Φ = 0.0100 T × π × (1.00 × 10^(-6) m)^2.

## Step 3: Perform the calculation
Φ = 0.0100 T × π × (1.00 × 10^(-6) m)^2 = 3.14159 × 10^(-14) Wb.

## Step 4: Compute Φ/Φ0
Divide the enclosed flux Φ by the single-electron flux quantum Φ0 = h/e = 4.136 × 10^(-15) Wb.

## Step 5: Calculate Φ/Φ0
Φ/Φ0 = (3.14159 × 10^(-14) Wb) / (4.136 × 10^(-15) Wb) = 7.600 × 10.

The total AB phase is given by Δφ = 2πΦ/Φ0.

Since the total AB phase is a multiple of 2π, we can simplify it to Δφ = 48.000 × 2π rad = 96.000π rad.

One full fringe corresponds to a phase difference of 2π. Therefore, the number of fringes is given by the total AB phase divided by 2π.

## Step 10: Calculate the fringe shift
fringe_shift = 96.000 / 2 = 48.

Using Φ0 = h/2e would be incorrect because it is the flux quantum for superconducting pairs, not single electrons. The problem specifically states that we are dealing with single electrons, so we should use the single-electron flux quantum Φ0 = h/e.
