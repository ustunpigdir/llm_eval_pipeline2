## Step 1: Physical Origin of the Casimir Effect
The Casimir effect arises from the difference in vacuum modes between two parallel plates. In the region between the plates, the modes with wavelengths smaller than the plate separation are excluded due to the boundary conditions imposed by the plates. This exclusion leads to a difference in the zero-point energy between the two regions, resulting in an attractive force between the plates. Regularization, such as the zeta-function, is needed to handle the infinite zero-point energy of the vacuum modes.

## Step 2: Evaluate the Casimir Pressure Magnitude
The Casimir pressure magnitude is given by $|P| = \pi^2\hbar c/(240 d^4)$. We need to evaluate this expression at $d = 100$ nm.

First, we need to convert the distance from nanometers to meters: $d = 100$ nm $= 100 \times 10^{-9}$ m $= 10^{-7}$ m.

Next, we plug in the values of the constants: $\hbar = 1.0545718 \times 10^{-34}$ J s, $c = 299792458$ m/s, and $\pi = 3.14159$.

We get:

$|P| = \pi^2\hbar c/(240 d^4) = (3.14159)^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 10^{-34} \times 299792458 / (240 \times (10^{-7})^4)$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 10^{-34} \times 299792458 / (240 \times 10^{-28})$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 299792458 / (240 \times 10^{4})$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 299792458 / 240000000$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 1249.743$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 1317.111$

$= 9.869604 \times 1317.111$

$= 12951.31$

$|P| = 12951.31$ Pa

The Casimir pressure is attractive.

## Step 3: Evaluate the Energy per Unit Area Magnitude
The energy per unit area magnitude is given by $|E/A| = \pi^2\hbar c/(720 d^3)$. We need to evaluate this expression at $d = 100$ nm.

First, we need to convert the distance from nanometers to meters: $d = 100$ nm $= 100 \times 10^{-9}$ m $= 10^{-7}$ m.

Next, we plug in the values of the constants: $\hbar = 1.0545718 \times 10^{-34}$ J s, $c = 299792458$ m/s, and $\pi = 3.14159$.

We get:

$|E/A| = \pi^2\hbar c/(720 d^3) = (3.14159)^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 10^{-34} \times 299792458 / (720 \times (10^{-7})^3)$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 10^{-34} \times 299792458 / (720 \times 10^{-21})$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 299792458 / (720 \times 10^{-17})$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 299792458 / 7200000000$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 1.0545718 \times 41.799$

$= 3.14159^2 \times 418.799$

$= 9.869604 \times 418.799$

$= 4101.51$

$|E/A| = 4101.51$ $\mu$J/m$^2$

## Step 4: Scaling Law and New Value for Pressure
If the distance $d$ doubles, the pressure magnitude will change according to the scaling law $|P| \propto 1/d^4$. Therefore, if $d$ doubles, the new pressure magnitude will be:

$|P|_{new} = |P|_{old} \times (d_{old}/d_{new})^4$

$= 12951.31 \times (100/200)^4$

$= 12951.31 \times (0.5)^4$

$= 12951.31 \times 0.0625$

$= 810.81$
