## Step 1: Photon Temperature Scaling
The photon temperature scales with redshift as $T \propto (1+z)$, since the energy of a photon is given by $E = h\nu = hc/\lambda = hc/T$, and the wavelength of a photon is inversely proportional to the redshift.

## Step 2: Evaluating T at Recombination
We can evaluate the photon temperature at recombination by using the given redshift $z = 1100$ and the current temperature $T_0 = 2.725$ K. We have:

$$T(z) = T_0 (1+z) = 2.725 (1+1100) = 2992.75 \text{ K}$$

## Step 3: Photon Number Density Scaling
The photon number density scales as $n \propto a^{-3}$, where $a$ is the scale factor. Since $a \propto (1+z)^{-1}$, we have:

$$n(z) = n_0 (1+z)^{-3}$$

## Step 4: Evaluating log10 of Number Density Ratio
We can evaluate the log10 of the ratio $n(z_{rec})/n_0$ by using the expression for $n(z)$:

$$\log_{10} \frac{n(z_{rec})}{n_0} = \log_{10} (1+z_{rec})^{-3} = -3 \log_{10} (1+1100)$$

## Step 5: Photon Energy Density Scaling
The photon energy density scales as $\rho \propto a^{-4}$, where $a$ is the scale factor. Since $a \propto (1+z)^{-1}$, we have:

$$\rho(z) = \rho_0 (1+z)^{-4}$$

## Step 6: Evaluating log10 of Energy Density Ratio
We can evaluate the log10 of the ratio $\rho(z_{rec})/\rho_0$ by using the expression for $\rho(z)$:

$$\log_{10} \frac{\rho(z_{rec})}{\rho_0} = \log_{10} (1+z_{rec})^{-4} = -4 \log_{10} (1+1100)$$

## Step 7: Why CMB Remains a Perfect Blackbody
The CMB remains a perfect blackbody as it redshifts because the photons are not interacting with matter, and therefore do not undergo any rethermalization.
