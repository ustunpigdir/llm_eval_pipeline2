**1. Time calculations:**

- Earth frame round-trip time:
  \[
  t_{Earth} = \frac{2 \times 6.00 \text{ ly}}{0.6c} = \frac{12.00}{0.6} \text{ yr} = 20.00 \text{ yr}
  \]

- Traveler's proper time:
  \[
  t_{proper} = \frac{6.00 \text{ ly}}{0.6c} \sqrt{1 - (0.6)^2} = \frac{6.00}{0.6} \sqrt{0.64} \text{ yr} \approx 8.00 \text{ yr}
  \]

**2. Doppler factor calculations:**

- Approach (Earth to traveler):
  \[
  k_{approach} = \sqrt{\frac{1 + 0.6}{1 - 0.6}} = \sqrt{\frac{1.6}{0.4}} = \sqrt{4} = 2
  \]

- Recession (Traveler to Earth):
  \[
  k_{recede} = \sqrt{\frac{1 - 0.6}{1 + 0.6}} = \sqrt{\frac{0.4}{1.6}} = \sqrt{0.25} = 0.5
  \]

**3. Pulse counting (k-calculus):**

- Outbound leg:
  \[
  \text{Pulses received} = k_{approach} \times t_{proper} = 2 \times 8.00 \text{ yr} = 16.00 \text{ yr}
  \]

- Return leg:
  \[
  \text{Pulses received} = k_{recede} \times t_{proper} = 0.5 \times 8.00 \text{ yr} = 4.00 \text{ yr}
  \]

- Total pulses received:
  \[
  16.00 \text{ yr} + 4.00 \text{ yr} = 20.00 \text{ yr}
  \]

**4. Asymmetry:**

The situation is not symmetric because the traveler experiences time dilation, while the Earth does not. The traveler's proper time is less than the Earth's time, leading to the traveler receiving fewer pulses on the return leg.
