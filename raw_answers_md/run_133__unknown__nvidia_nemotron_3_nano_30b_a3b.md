The photon temperature of a black‑body radiation field scales with the cosmic scale factor as  

\[
T \propto \frac{1}{a}\;,
\]

so for a redshift \(z\) (where \(a=1/(1+z)\))  

\[
T(z)=T_0\,(1+z).
\]

With \(T_0 = 2.725\;\text{K}\) and \(z_{\rm rec}=1100\),

\[
T_{\rm rec}=2.725\times(1+1100)=2.725\times1101
          = 3000.225\;\text{K}\;\approx\;3000.2\;\text{K}.
\]

The photon number density scales as  

\[
n\propto a^{-3}\propto (1+z)^3,
\]

hence  

\[
\frac{n(z_{\rm rec})}{n_0}=(1+z_{\rm rec})^{3}=1101^{3}.
\]

Taking the base‑10 logarithm,

\[
\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{n(z_{\rm rec})}{n_0}\right)
   =3\log_{10}(1+z_{\rm rec})
   =3\log_{10}(1101)
   \approx 3\times 3.04184
   \approx 9.13 .
\]

The energy density of the photon gas is  

\[
\rho \propto a^{-4}\propto (1+z)^4,
\]

so  

\[
\frac{\rho(z_{\rm rec})}{\rho_0}=(1+z_{\rm rec})^{4}=1101^{4},
\]

and  

\[
\log_{10}\!\left(\frac{\rho(z_{\rm rec})}{\rho_0}\right)
   =4\log_{10}(1+z_{\rm rec})
   =4\times 3.04184
   \approx 12.17 .
\]

The extra factor of \((1+z)\) in the energy‑density scaling arises because each photon’s energy redshifts as \(E\propto h\nu\propto 1/a\propto (1+z)\). Thus  

\[
\rho = n\langle E\rangle \propto (1+z)^3 \times (1+z) = (1+z)^4 .
\]

After recombination the photon distribution retains its Planck form because the expansion is adiabatic and photon number is conserved; there are no subsequent interactions that would distort the spectrum, so the CMB remains an almost perfect blackbody.

\[
\begin{aligned}
\mathrm{T\_rec\_K} &= 3000.2 \\
\mathrm{log10\_number\_ratio} &= 9.13 \\
\mathrm{log10\_energy\_ratio} &= 12.17
\end{aligned}
\]
